# SPT - Annual Tax Return Filing

**TITLE: SPT - Annual Tax Return (Surat Pemberitahuan Tahunan)**
**CATEGORY: Tax Compliance**
**PRIORITY: HIGH** - Mandatory annual obligation for all NPWP holders.

---

## What is SPT?

**SPT** (Surat Pemberitahuan Tahunan) is Indonesia's Annual Tax Return. All NPWP holders must file by:
- **Personal:** March 31st each year
- **Corporate:** April 30th each year

## SPT Types

### SPT 1770 - Self-Employed / Business
For individuals with:
- Business income
- Multiple income sources
- Foreign income
- Capital gains

### SPT 1770 S - Employee (Simplified)
For employees with:
- Single employer
- Gross income > IDR 60M/year
- Standard deductions

### SPT 1770 SS - Employee (Super Simple)
For employees with:
- Gross income < IDR 60M/year
- Single employer only
- No other income

### SPT 1771 - Corporate
For all companies:
- PT PMA
- PT Lokal
- CV

## Filing Process

### Personal SPT:

1. **Gather documents:**
   - Form 1721-A1 from employer (bukti potong)
   - Bank interest certificates
   - Investment statements
   - Asset/liability list

2. **Calculate taxes:**
   - Total income - deductions = taxable income
   - Apply progressive rates (5% - 35%)
   - Subtract withholding already paid

3. **File online:**
   - Login to djponline.pajak.go.id
   - Complete e-Filing form
   - Submit by March 31st

4. **Pay if underpaid:**
   - Generate billing code
   - Pay via bank/ATM/e-wallet

### Corporate SPT:

1. **Prepare financial statements:**
   - Balance sheet
   - Income statement
   - General ledger

2. **Calculate corporate tax:**
   - Revenue - expenses = profit
   - 22% corporate tax rate (standard)
   - 11% for small enterprises (< IDR 4.8B revenue)

3. **File via e-SPT or Coretax:**
   - Submit by April 30th
   - Attach financial statements

## Zero/Dormant Company SPT

### What is it?
For companies with no operational activity in the fiscal year.

### Requirements:
- Still must file SPT (NIL return)
- Report zero revenue/expenses
- Confirm dormant status

### Why cheaper?
- No complex calculations needed
- No financial statement audit required
- Simple NIL declaration

## FAQ

**Q: What if I have no income?**
A: Still must file NIL SPT. Report zero income.

**Q: Can I file late?**
A: Yes, but penalty applies. File ASAP to minimize fines.

**Q: Do I need accountant?**
A: Personal SPT often DIY. Corporate SPT recommended with professional.

**Q: What documents to keep?**
A: Keep 10 years: invoices, receipts, bank statements, contracts.

**Q: KITAS holder - must I file?**
A: If you have NPWP and Indonesian income, yes. Remote workers earning only foreign income may be exempt.
