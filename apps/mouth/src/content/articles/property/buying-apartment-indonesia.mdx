# Buying an Apartment in Indonesia 2025: Foreigner's Guide

Apartments (condos) offer one of the clearest legal paths for foreigners to own property in Indonesia. Here's your complete guide.

## Can Foreigners Buy Apartments?

<InfoCard variant="note" title="Yes, With Requirements">
Foreigners can own apartments in Indonesia under certain conditions:
- Must have valid **KITAS or KITAP**
- Property must meet **minimum price** requirements
- Must be in a **strata title** building approved for foreign ownership
- Ownership is through **Hak Milik Satuan Rumah Susun** (HMSRS) or **Hak Pakai** strata
</InfoCard>

---

## Strata Title Ownership

When you buy an apartment, you get:

### What You Own
- **The unit**: Your specific apartment space
- **Share of common property**: Proportional ownership of shared facilities
- **Membership rights**: In the building management (P3SRS)

### Common Property Includes
- Land the building sits on
- Structure and roof
- Corridors and lobbies
- Swimming pools and gyms
- Parking structures
- Gardens and common areas

## What to Check

### Building Due Diligence
- Developer track record
- Building completion status
- Strata title issued/pending
- Management company quality
- Foreign ownership allowed

### Unit Due Diligence
- Title status (SHMSRS issued)
- Any encumbrances
- Handover condition
- Inclusions (furniture, etc.)

<InfoCard variant="tip" title="New vs Resale">
**New from developer**: May get strata title later (PPJB first)
**Resale**: Should have strata title ready for transfer
</InfoCard>

## Ongoing Costs

### Monthly/Annual Fees

| Cost | Typical Range | Notes |
|------|--------------|-------|
| Service charge | IDR 50-100k/sqm/month | Building maintenance |
| Sinking fund | IDR 10-30k/sqm/month | Major repairs reserve |
| PBB (property tax) | 0.1-0.3% of value/year | Annual tax |
| Insurance | Varies | Personal contents |

### If Renting Out
- Rental income tax: 10% of gross rent
- Management fees: 10-20% of rent
