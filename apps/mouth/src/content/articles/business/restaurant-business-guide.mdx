# Starting a Restaurant in Indonesia 2026: Complete Guide for Foreigners

Opening a restaurant in Indonesia as a foreigner is absolutely possible, but requires proper structure and licensing. Here's your complete guide.

## Can Foreigners Open Restaurants?

<InfoCard variant="note" title="Yes, But With Requirements">
Foreigners can own restaurants in Indonesia through a **PT PMA** (foreign investment company). Key requirements:
- Minimum investment: IDR 10 billion (~USD 625,000)
- Cannot be small warung/street food
- Must be formal restaurant establishment
- Need multiple permits and licenses
</InfoCard>

## Liquor License (SIUP-MB)

If you want to serve alcohol:

<InfoCard variant="warning" title="Alcohol License Is Complex">
Liquor licenses in Indonesia are:
- Heavily regulated
- Location-dependent (some areas prohibit)
- Expensive to obtain
- Subject to periodic review

In Bali, licenses are more accessible. In other areas, may be impossible.
</InfoCard>

### License Types

| Type | Description | Where |
|------|-------------|-------|
| SIUP-MB A | Alcoholic beverages (beer, wine, spirits) | Hotels, restaurants |
| SIUP-MB B | Beer only | Bars, restaurants |
| SIUP-MB C | Retail sale | Shops with license |

## Staffing Requirements

### Indonesian Staff
- Must hire Indonesian workers
- Register with BPJS (health and employment insurance)
- Follow minimum wage (UMR) by region
- Provide employment contracts

### Foreign Staff
- Limited to certain positions (chef, manager)
- Requires RPTKA and KITAS
- Must train Indonesian counterpart
- Additional costs (DKP-TKA)

<InfoCard variant="tip" title="Hiring Foreign Chefs">
Foreign chefs are common in Indonesia but require:
- Work permit (RPTKA + KITAS)
- Justification why Indonesian chef not suitable
- Indonesian sous chef to learn from them
</InfoCard>

## Common Challenges

1. **High investment minimum** - IDR 10B is substantial
2. **Location competition** - Premium spots expensive
3. **License complexity** - Multiple agencies involved
4. **Staff management** - Training and retention
5. **Seasonal business** - Especially in tourist areas
6. **Alcohol restrictions** - Limiting in some areas
