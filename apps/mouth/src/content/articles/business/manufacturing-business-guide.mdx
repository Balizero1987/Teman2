# Manufacturing Business in Indonesia 2025: Foreign Investor Guide

Indonesia offers competitive manufacturing with large labor pool, strategic location, and growing domestic market. Here's how to set up production operations.

## Why Manufacture in Indonesia?

### Advantages
- **Labor force**: 130+ million workers
- **Competitive costs**: Lower than China, Thailand
- **Young population**: Median age 29
- **Strategic location**: Access to ASEAN markets
- **Natural resources**: Raw materials available
- **Growing market**: 270 million consumers

### Challenges
- Infrastructure varies by region
- Bureaucracy can be slow
- Labor regulations favor workers
- Minimum wages rising annually

## Labor Requirements

### Employment Basics

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Minimum Wage | Provincial (UMK) - varies IDR 2-5M/month |
| BPJS Kesehatan | Mandatory health insurance |
| BPJS Ketenagakerjaan | Mandatory employment insurance |
| Working Hours | 40 hours/week maximum |
| Overtime | 1.5x-4x base rate |
| Severance | Significant obligations |

<InfoCard variant="warning" title="Labor Laws Favor Workers">
Indonesian labor law provides strong worker protections:
- Difficult to terminate permanent employees
- Severance can be substantial (up to 35 months)
- Unions are common in manufacturing
- Strikes are protected
</InfoCard>

### Foreign Workers

Limited to:
- Technical specialists
- Management positions
- Training roles

Requirements:
- RPTKA approval
- DKP-TKA fees
- Indonesian counterpart training

## Export Manufacturing

### Bonded Zone Benefits

For export-focused manufacturing:
- **Import duty deferral** on raw materials
- **VAT exemption** on imported inputs
- **Faster customs** processing
- **Simplified procedures**

### KITE Facility

Kemudahan Impor Tujuan Ekspor:
- Duty/tax suspension for export production
- Available outside bonded zones
- Requires export commitment
- Regular auditing

---

## Environmental Compliance

### AMDAL Requirements

For large manufacturing:
- Environmental Impact Assessment
- Before construction begins
- Community consultation required
- Ongoing monitoring obligations

### Waste Management

- Proper waste disposal systems
- Hazardous waste handling if applicable
- Water treatment if required
- Emissions monitoring
