### Export License

For exports, you need:
- **ET (Eksportir Terdaftar)** - Registered Exporter for certain goods
- Some products require specific export permits
- Most products can be exported with just NIB

## Business Structure

### PT PMA for Trading

Foreign-owned trading companies can:
- Import goods for resale
- Export Indonesian products
- Act as distributor
- Wholesale to retailers

<InfoCard variant="note" title="Investment Requirement">
Trading PT PMA requires:
- Minimum **IDR 10 billion** investment
- Paid-up capital **IDR 2.5 billion**
- Proper warehouse facilities (for certain goods)
- Registered import/export codes
</InfoCard>

## Customs & Duties

### Import Duties

| Category | Duty Rate | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Raw materials | 0-5% | Often lower |
| Semi-finished | 5-15% | Varies by product |
| Finished goods | 10-40% | Higher protection |
| Luxury items | 20-200% | Additional luxury tax |

### Other Import Charges
- **VAT (PPN)**: 11% on CIF + duty
- **Income Tax (PPh 22)**: 2.5% with API, 7.5% without
- **Luxury Tax (PPnBM)**: On certain goods

<InfoCard variant="tip" title="HS Code Research">
Research your product's **HS Code** (Harmonized System) to understand:
- Applicable duty rate
- Import restrictions
- Required permits
- Documentation needs

Use Indonesia's INSW portal for HS code lookup.
</InfoCard>

## Product Restrictions

### Import Restrictions

Some products require additional permits:
- **Food/Beverages**: BPOM registration
- **Electronics**: SNI certification
- **Cosmetics**: BPOM + Halal
- **Pharmaceuticals**: Strict licensing
- **Textiles**: Quota systems may apply

### Prohibited Items
- Drugs/narcotics
- Weapons (without license)
- Certain agricultural products
- Products violating IP rights
- Items against public morals

## Export Opportunities

### Top Indonesian Exports
1. Palm oil products
2. Coal and minerals
3. Textiles and garments
4. Rubber products
5. Electronics
6. Processed food
7. Furniture and handicrafts

### Export Requirements
- ET (Registered Exporter) for certain goods
- Export documentation (invoice, packing list, etc.)
- Pre-shipment inspection (some products)
- Certificate of origin (for preferential rates)

## Bonded Zones

<GlossaryTerm
  term="Kawasan Berikat"
  definition="Bonded zones are designated areas where goods can be imported, stored, processed, and re-exported with duty suspension."
/>

### Benefits of Bonded Zones
- Defer import duties until goods enter domestic market
- VAT and income tax facilities
- Streamlined customs procedures
- Ideal for manufacturing for export

## Freight and Logistics

### Key Ports
- **Jakarta (Tanjung Priok)** - Largest, most congested
- **Surabaya (Tanjung Perak)** - East Java hub
- **Semarang (Tanjung Emas)** - Central Java
- **Medan (Belawan)** - North Sumatra

### Air Cargo
- **Soekarno-Hatta (CGK)** - Main international
- **Ngurah Rai (DPS)** - Bali
- **Juanda (SUB)** - Surabaya

### Freight Forwarders
Work with established forwarders who can:
- Handle customs clearance
- Manage documentation
- Arrange inland transport
- Provide bonded warehouse

## Common Mistakes

<InfoCard variant="error" title="Avoid These Errors">
1. **Wrong API type** - API-U vs API-P must match activity
2. **Ignoring product regulations** - Some goods need special permits
3. **Undervaluing goods** - Customs penalties are severe
4. **No proper documentation** - Delays and extra costs
5. **Wrong HS code** - Different duties, potential penalties
6. **Ignoring quotas/restrictions** - Some products limited
</InfoCard>
