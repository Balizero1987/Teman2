# Healthcare System Indonesia 2025: Expat Guide

Healthcare in Indonesia ranges from basic clinics to world-class private hospitals. Here's what expats need to know.

## Healthcare Overview

<InfoCard variant="note" title="Healthcare Landscape">
**Indonesia's healthcare system:**

- **Public hospitals** - Affordable but often crowded
- **Private hospitals** - Better service, higher costs
- **International hospitals** - Western standards, premium prices
- **Clinics** - For minor issues, abundant everywhere

Most expats use **private/international hospitals** for quality care.
</InfoCard>

## Medical Costs

### Typical Expenses

| Service | Public | Private | International |
|---------|--------|---------|---------------|
| GP consultation | IDR 50-100K | IDR 300-500K | IDR 500K-1M |
| Specialist | IDR 100-200K | IDR 500K-1M | IDR 1-2M |
| Emergency visit | IDR 200-500K | IDR 1-3M | IDR 3-10M |
| Hospital room/night | IDR 200-500K | IDR 1-3M | IDR 3-8M |
| Minor surgery | IDR 2-5M | IDR 10-30M | IDR 30-100M |
| Major surgery | IDR 20-50M | IDR 50-200M | IDR 200M+ |

### Payment Methods

| Method | Accepted |
|--------|----------|
| Cash | Always |
| Credit/debit card | Private/International hospitals |
| Insurance direct billing | Major hospitals with agreements |
| Transfer | Usually accepted |

<InfoCard variant="warning" title="Payment Often Upfront">
Unlike some countries, Indonesian hospitals often require:

- **Deposit** before admission
- **Payment** before discharge
- **Cash** for smaller clinics

Always have emergency funds or insurance with direct billing!
</InfoCard>

## Emergency Situations

### What to Do

| Emergency | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Life threatening | Call 118 (ambulance) or go directly to ER |
| Serious | Go to nearest hospital with ER |
| Moderate | Private hospital outpatient |
| Minor | Local clinic |

### Emergency Numbers

| Service | Number |
|---------|--------|
| General emergency | 112 |
| Ambulance | 118 |
| BIMC Bali | +62 361 761263 |
| International SOS | Check your policy |

<InfoCard variant="warning" title="Ambulance Reality">
**Important to know:**

- Ambulance response can be slow
- Traffic can delay arrival
- Often faster to take taxi/Grab
- Know nearest hospital location
- Have hospital numbers saved

In emergencies, don't wait - go directly if possible.
</InfoCard>

## Pharmacy (Apotek)

### Medications

<InfoCard variant="note" title="Pharmacy Tips">
**Good to know:**

- Many medications available without prescription
- Common medicines much cheaper than Western countries
- International brands and local generics available
- Pharmacist can often recommend for minor issues
- Bring prescription from doctor for controlled substances
</InfoCard>
