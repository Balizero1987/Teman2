## Getting a SIM Card

### Tourist SIM (Airport/Shop)

<InfoCard variant="tip" title="Easiest Option">
Available at airports, malls, and phone shops:
- **Pre-registered** (no passport copy needed)
- **Ready to use** immediately
- **Tourist packages** with data
- Cost: IDR 50-150K with data
</InfoCard>

### Regular SIM Registration

Since 2019, Indonesia requires **SIM card registration** with ID.

### IMEI Registration

<InfoCard variant="warning" title="Important for Foreign Phones">
Foreign phones must register their **IMEI** to work with Indonesian SIMs beyond 90 days.

**Options:**
1. Register at airport arrival (free for first phone)
2. Register online via Bea Cukai (customs)
3. Pay tax for additional devices (~IDR 2.5M)

Without registration, your SIM will stop working after 90 days.
</InfoCard>

## Troubleshooting

### Slow Connection?

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Congestion | Switch to less busy provider |
| Location | Move closer to window/router |
| Throttling | Check data usage, buy package |
| VPN needed | Use quality VPN service |

### VPN in Indonesia

<InfoCard variant="note" title="VPN Usage">
VPNs are **not illegal** in Indonesia but:
- Some sites are blocked (Reddit, etc.)
- Streaming may require VPN
- Quality VPNs recommended
- Free VPNs often slow

Popular options: ExpressVPN, NordVPN, Surfshark
</InfoCard>

## Quick Tips

1. **Buy SIM at airport** - Easiest for tourists
2. **Telkomsel for travel** - Best coverage outside cities
3. **XL/Tri for cities** - Better value if staying put
4. **Top up via apps** - GoPay, OVO, Dana work
5. **Check MyTelkomsel app** - Easy package management
6. **Register IMEI early** - Don't wait until blocked
7. **Have backup** - Always have mobile data as backup
