## What to Check

<InfoCard variant="warning" title="Before Signing">
**Always verify:**
- Water pressure and heating
- Internet speed test
- Air conditioning works
- Kitchen appliances
- Security (locks, safe area)
- Noise levels
- Parking (if needed)
- Generator/backup power
- Pest situation
- Landlord responsiveness
</InfoCard>

## Rental Agreement Tips

### Key Terms to Include

| Term | What to Check |
|------|---------------|
| Duration | Start/end dates clear |
| Rent amount | Total and payment schedule |
| Deposit | Amount and return conditions |
| Utilities | Who pays what |
| Maintenance | Responsibility for repairs |
| Termination | Notice period, penalties |
| Inventory | List of included items |

<InfoCard variant="tip" title="Get It in Writing">
Even informal rentals should have:
- Written agreement (can be simple)
- Payment receipts
- Condition documentation
- Contact details for landlord

Indonesian contracts are enforceable - get one!
</InfoCard>

## Furnished vs Unfurnished

### Bali/Tourist Areas

Most rentals are **fully furnished**:
- Bed, mattress, linens
- Living room furniture
- Kitchen equipped
- Air conditioning
- Hot water
- Often includes WiFi

### Jakarta/Cities

Mix of options:
- Serviced apartments: fully furnished
- Regular apartments: basic furnishing
- Houses: may be unfurnished

## Long-Term Savings

| Term | Typical Discount |
|------|------------------|
| Monthly | Full price |
| 3 months | 5-10% off |
| 6 months | 15-25% off |
| 12 months | 25-40% off |

<InfoCard variant="tip" title="Negotiate!">
Tips for better rates:
- Ask directly (don't accept first price)
- Offer longer term
- Pay upfront if possible
- Low season = better deals
- Direct with owner = no agent fee
</InfoCard>
