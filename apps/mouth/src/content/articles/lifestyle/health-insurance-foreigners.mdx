## BPJS Kesehatan

### What is BPJS?

<InfoCard variant="note" title="Mandatory for KITAS Holders">
**BPJS Kesehatan** is Indonesia's national health insurance. It's **mandatory** for:
- Indonesian citizens
- Foreigners with KITAS working 6+ months

Your employer must register you or you register yourself.
</InfoCard>

### BPJS Classes

| Class | Monthly Premium | Room Type | Notes |
|-------|-----------------|-----------|-------|
| Class 1 | IDR 150,000 | VIP single | Best |
| Class 2 | IDR 100,000 | Semi-private | Good |
| Class 3 | IDR 42,000 | Ward | Basic |

### BPJS Coverage
- Outpatient care
- Inpatient hospitalization
- Surgery
- Medication (formulary)
- Emergency care
- Specialist referrals

### BPJS Limitations
- Long wait times common
- Quality varies by hospital
- Referral system required
- Some medications not covered
- Premium hospitals often not included

## International Insurance

### Why Many Expats Choose International

1. **Hospital choice** - Access to premium private hospitals
2. **No referrals** - Go directly to specialist
3. **Global coverage** - Covered when traveling
4. **English support** - Easy communication
5. **Evacuation** - Medical evacuation if needed

## Pre-existing Conditions

<InfoCard variant="warning" title="Disclosure is Important">
Most international insurers will:
- Ask about pre-existing conditions
- May exclude them initially
- Offer coverage after waiting period
- Premium may be higher

**Never hide conditions** - claims can be denied if undisclosed.
</InfoCard>
