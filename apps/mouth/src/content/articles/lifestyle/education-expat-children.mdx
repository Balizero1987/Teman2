## Important Considerations

### Visa Requirements

<InfoCard variant="warning" title="Legal Status">
Children must have proper visa status to attend school:

**Required:**
- Dependent KITAS (linked to parent's work permit)
- Student KITAS (for study purposes)
- Second Home Visa (children included)

**VOA/Visa-Free:** Generally not sufficient for school enrollment

Check visa requirements before enrolling!
</InfoCard>

## Tips for Choosing

<InfoCard variant="tip" title="What to Consider">
**Beyond academics:**

1. **Location** - Commute time matters
2. **Community** - Family involvement level
3. **Facilities** - Sports, arts, technology
4. **Class size** - Teacher ratios
5. **After-school** - Activities available
6. **Languages** - Indonesian, mother tongue
7. **Support** - Learning differences, counseling
8. **Future** - University preparation

Visit multiple schools before deciding!
</InfoCard>

## Homeschooling Option

### Is It Legal?

<InfoCard variant="note" title="Homeschooling in Indonesia">
Homeschooling is **legal** in Indonesia with proper registration.

**Requirements:**
- Register with local education office
- Follow approved curriculum
- Regular assessments
- Documentation maintained

Many expat families combine homeschooling with local activities and tutors.
</InfoCard>
