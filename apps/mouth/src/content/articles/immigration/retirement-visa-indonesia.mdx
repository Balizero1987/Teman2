# Retirement Visa in Indonesia 2025: Complete Guide

Indonesia offers a retirement visa (KITAS Lansia / Index 319) for foreigners who want to enjoy their retirement in beautiful locations like Bali, without working.

## Retirement Visa Overview

<InfoCard variant="note" title="What is the Retirement Visa?">
The retirement visa is a **1-year KITAS** for foreigners aged 55+ who can prove:
- Sufficient income/pension
- Health insurance
- Indonesian accommodation

It's **non-work** - you cannot be employed or run a business.
</InfoCard>

---

## Requirements

### Age Requirement
- **Minimum age**: 55 years old

### Financial Requirements

| Requirement | Amount |
|-------------|--------|
| Pension/passive income | USD 1,500+ per month |
| Proof period | Last 6-12 months |
| Bank statement | Showing regular deposits |

### Other Requirements
- Health insurance valid in Indonesia
- Indonesian accommodation (owned, leased, or rented)
- Sponsor (agent or individual)

## Rights and Limitations

### What You CAN Do
- Live in Indonesia long-term
- Travel in and out (multiple entry)
- Open bank account
- Rent property
- Sponsor spouse/dependents
- Enjoy retirement!

### What You CANNOT Do
- Work (any kind)
- Run a business
- Invest in Indonesian company as director
- Engage in commercial activities

<InfoCard variant="warning" title="No Work Allowed">
The retirement visa is strictly non-work. Any employment or business activity violates the visa conditions and can result in deportation.
</InfoCard>

---

## Extension and Renewal

### Annual Extension
- Apply 30 days before expiry
- Same documents (updated)
- Similar process to initial application

### Path to KITAP
After **5 consecutive years** on KITAS, you may apply for **KITAP** (permanent stay permit):
- Valid for 5 years
- Renewable
- More stability

---

## Health Insurance

Required for retirement visa:

### Coverage Requirements
- Valid in Indonesia
- Adequate medical coverage
- Emergency evacuation preferred

### Options
- International health insurance
- Indonesian insurance (BPJS not sufficient alone)
- Travel insurance (for initial period)
