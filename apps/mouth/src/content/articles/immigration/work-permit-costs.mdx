### 2. Agent/Processing Fees

Most companies use immigration agents. Typical fees:

| Service | Cost Range (IDR) | Notes |
|---------|------------------|-------|
| Full KITAS processing | 15-25 million | All-inclusive |
| RPTKA only | 5-8 million | If handling KITAS yourself |
| Extension processing | 8-15 million | Simpler than new application |
| Rush processing | +30-50% | Not always possible |

<InfoCard variant="tip" title="Agent Selection">
Choose agents who:
- Have clear pricing (no hidden fees)
- Provide written service agreement
- Have verifiable track record
- Are responsive to questions
</InfoCard>

## Who Pays?

Legally, the **employer** is responsible for most work permit costs. In practice:

### Employer Usually Pays
- DKP-TKA (mandatory employer obligation)
- RPTKA processing
- Agent fees

### Often Negotiated
- KITAS fees
- Extension costs
- Document preparation

<InfoCard variant="warning" title="Check Your Contract">
Before accepting a job, clarify:
- Who pays for initial work permit?
- Who pays for extensions?
- What happens if you leave early?
- Are costs deducted from salary?

Get it in writing in your employment contract.
</InfoCard>

## Saving Money

Tips to reduce costs:

1. **Use company in-house** - Some large companies have immigration staff
2. **Multi-year permits** - 2-year permits cost less per year than 1-year
3. **Bundle with others** - Agents may discount for multiple workers
4. **Compare agents** - Prices vary significantly
5. **Prepare documents properly** - Avoid re-processing fees
